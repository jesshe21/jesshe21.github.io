About Me

I grew up in the cozy town of Rochester, MN, sandwiched between Mayo Clinic and IBM—the two recognizable names within 50 miles. With my mom working in medical research at Mayo and my dad working as an engineer at IBM, I knew I had to explore something different. After high school, I moved 1,800 miles away to attend college at Berkeley.

Of my experiences at Berkeley, I particularly enjoyed teaching a semester-long origami course, interning abroad in Ireland, and bombarding Sproul Plaza passersby with free Exo cricket bars and magazines as a campus ambassador for The Economist. I also joined several student organizations that jointly gave me both a dependable circle of friends and an opportunity to explore disparate career paths.

Since Berkeley, I've been working in finance in San Francisco. If you’d like to hop on a call or meet for coffee, I would love to hear from you! You can reach me via email at jesshe21@berkeley.edu or use the contact form on this site.
